    How it works

    Momentum members can use our Democracy Review platform to: 1) Make proposals, 2) Make comments, 3) Propose amendments, 4) Make nominations, and 5) Vote on our final submissions.

    1) Make Proposals

    Proposals are any contributions which put forward any new ideas by members. Members can make one proposal per theme, and they can also be submitted by local Momentum groups, as well as by the National Coordinating Group (NCG) and the Members’ Council. Proposals must be made within certain deadlines (the deadline to make proposals for Track 2 submissions is February 1st at midnight). Before making a proposal, please see our recommendations for proposers.

    2) Make Comments

    The purpose of comments is to allow members to express support, disagreement, or to share experiences and thoughts which are relevant to any given proposal. Any disagreements should be expressed in terms which are respectful and comradely.

    3) Propose Amendments

    Momentum members have the option of suggesting amendments to all proposals, including ones made by other members and groups, ones made by the National Coordinating Group (NCG) and Members’ Council, as well as Momentum’s recommended submissions for Track 1 (BAME Labour, Young Labour, and Women’s Conference). In all cases, the original proposers shall have authority over whether or not to accept amendments. for Track 2 submissions, the amendments shall be allowed anytime until midnight Thursday 1st February.

    4) Make Nominations

    The purpose of nominating in the Digital Platform is to give a clear indication to Momentum’s NCG which are the most popular proposals. Each Momentum member has one nomination in each of the 9 themes of Track 2 and Track 3 in the Democracy Review. They can nominate at anytime between Thursday 25th January and Thursday 1st February for Track 2 submissions, and anytime up until 23rd March for Track 3, and they can change their nominations as many times as they want to between those dates.

    5) Final OMOV Ballots

    The most popular proposals in each category will be submitted to Momentum’s National Coodinating Group (NCG). The final versions will then put to an OMOV Ballot of members, before being approved officially by Momentum.

    Timeline

    For a full timeline, please click here. We acknowledge that the deadlines for Track 2 are extremely tight, and would like to apologise for this. The reason is the importance of being able to circulate finalised submissions in time for activists to be able to pass them through CLPs before the 23rd March deadline.
