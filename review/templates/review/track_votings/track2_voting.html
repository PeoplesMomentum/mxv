A) Membership Participation and Involvement



Charter of Members’ Rights

SUMMARY: Labour should adopt a Charter of Members’ Rights to support and expand members’ rights by focusing on transparency, accountability, participation, capacity building, and disciplinary justice as set out in the Chakrabarti Report.

Full proposal | Source: Momentum’s National Coordinating Group, Chakrabarti Report.

Code of Ethics

SUMMARY: Labour should adopt a Code of Ethics, which aims to ensure that all members of the Labour Party uphold and abide by the values and principles it wishes to promote in society. It includes a commitment for all party staff to abide by the principle of impartiality and accountability to democratically elected representatives, as well as for all party actors to avoid actions which are detrimental to the party.

Full proposal | Source: Momentum’s National Coordinating Group.

Labour Party Ombudsperson

SUMMARY: Labour should have an Ombudsperson, who would be in charge of ensuring the implementation of the Charter of Members’ Rights and Code of Ethics. The Ombudsperson would be independent and impartial, and would report to the NEC on the pattern of complaints received.

Full proposal | Source: Momentum NCG.

Membership of Other Organisations

SUMMARY: The existing wording on membership of organisations other than official Labour Party groups needs to be clarified, by clearly emphasising that only membership of organisations which have principles, values, objectives or methods of organising which are demonstrably incompatible with those of the Labour Party shall be considered problematic and grounds for making someone ineligible for membership status.

Full proposal | Source: Momentum NCG, based on proposals and representations made by members through My Momentum.

A Democratic Selection Process for the 21st Century

SUMMARY: The Labour Party should reform the way sitting MPs are reselected. Key points include:

  • New candidates should be able to seek out nominations as well as the sitting MP.
  • Sitting MPs should be required to receive nominations from branches representing over two thirds of the membership as well as over two thirds of affiliate branches in order to get reselected automatically.

Full proposal | Source: A proposal by Dan, as well as a rule change passed by Brighton Pavillion, Worthing West and Bristol West CLPs, which is due to be debated at this year’s Labour Party Conference.

C) Disability Labour and the Representation of Disabled Members

Please only answer this question if you are disabled.

SUMMARY: Labour should support reforms to Disability Labour and make additional reforms to improve the representation of disabled people in the party. Key points include:

  • Automatic membership of Disability Labour for all disabled party members.
  • Scrapping of the registration fee for joining Disability Labour.
  • Election of the Disability Labour Executive by One Member One Vote.
  • The Labour Party to provide Disability Labour with regularly updated data regarding its membership.
  • One staff member accountable to Disability Labour and responsible for promoting accessibility through the party.
  • Disability Officers to be Executive Officers in CLPs with full voting rights.

Full proposal | Source: It is based partly on proposals by Caroline, Fran, Adrie, Mick, and Ian.

D) Constituency Labour Party Governance

SUMMARY: Labour should reform the governance of Constituency Labour Parties (CLPs). Key points include:

  • CLP Executives to always include an Online Communications Officer, a Political Education and Training Officer and a Trade Union Liaison Officer (TULO), with full voting rights.
  • CLP Executives to always include representatives of BAME, LGBTQ and disabled people, with full voting rights, or a combined equalities officer if that isn’t possible.
  • CLP Executives to allow job shares for positions.
  • The Labour Party to increase funding to CLPs.
  • Efforts to ensure that all members of the CLP Exec are fully informed of guidance notes for CLPs produced by Disability Equalities Act Labour to improve accessibility.

Full proposal | Source: This is based in part on proposals by the Members' Council, Caroline, Lydia, Andy, Ian, Jessica, as well as Heather on behalf of the Campaign for Labour Party Democracy.

E) Recruitment of Members

SUMMARY: The Labour Party should support reforms to ensure high levels of recruitment of members in the future. Key points include:

  • Maintain the category of registered supporters and their right to vote in leadership campaigns.
  • Late freeze dates during leadership campaigns to allow newly enthused members of the public the chance to sign up and vote.
  • Integration of recruitment with community organising, in cooperation with the Community Organising Unit.
  • Consultation with BAME Labour, LGBT Labour, and Disability Labour in the development of recruitment strategies.

Full proposal | Source: This proposal is partly based on contributions from Lee, Joshua and A Porter.

F) Gender Representation

SUMMARY: The Labour Party should support reforms to improve the representation of women in the party. Key points include:

  • All CLPs shall be required to form Women’s Forums when there is demand from female members.
  • A standalone Women’s Conference to be held in Spring, to which each CLP shall be eligible to send at least one woman delegate.
  • Annual Women’s Conference to elect a Women’s Committee, which shall be the institutional representative of female party members.

Full proposal | Source: The proposal is based on proposals by Labour Women Leading, the Campaign for Labour Party Democracy, Jean and Jody.

G) Social Media

  • The renovation of Membersnet to convert it into an interactive hub.
